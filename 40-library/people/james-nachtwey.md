# James Nachtwey

> "I have been a witness, and these pictures are my testimony. The events I have recorded should not be forgotten and must not be repeated."

**James Nachtwey** (born March 14, 1948) is an American photojournalist and war photographer, widely regarded as one of the greatest documentary photographers of his generation. Often compared to Robert Capa for the 20th century, Nachtwey has spent over four decades documenting wars, conflicts, disasters, and socio-political issues across the globe.

### TED Prize Wish (2007)

Nachtwey's wish: *"There's a vital story that needs to be told, and I wish for TED to help me gain access to it and then to help me come up with innovative and exciting ways to use news photography in the digital era."*

**Result:** Documentation of extensively drug-resistant tuberculosis (XDR-TB) worldwide — unveiled at XDRTB.org using novel display technologies and internet distribution.

## Philosophy & Style

### Influences
- **Larry Burrows** — *Life* magazine photographer in Vietnam; opened Nachtwey's eyes to "the power of pictures" during his Dartmouth years
- **Robert Capa** — The mid-20th century war photographer; Nachtwey considered the Capa equivalent for the late 20th century

### Artistic Approach
- **Witness testimony** — Photography as ethical documentation and historical record
- **Brutal compassion** — Unflinching portrayal of suffering combined with deep human empathy
- **Silent witness** — Letting images speak; minimal intervention
- **Continuum of history** — Each photo as a "fragment of memory" within larger historical narratives

### On His Work
> "The events I have recorded should not be forgotten and must not be repeated."

## Legacy & Influence

- **The Bang-Bang Club** — Associate member during South Africa's transition period
- **Generational influence** — Widely cited as inspiration by younger conflict photographers
- **Digital innovation** — TED Prize work pioneered new distribution methods for documentary photography
- **Ethical photojournalism** — Standard-bearer for witness-based, non-exploitative conflict documentation
